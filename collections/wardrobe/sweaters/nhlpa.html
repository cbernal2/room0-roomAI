NHLPA Crewneck

Dad's Memory

"I can't remember which Roots I got this from. Maybe STC. Roots clothing last forever."

STC stands for Scarborough Town Center, one of the largest regional shopping centers in Toronto's east end.

It is strategically positioned next to Canada's busiest highway, the 401, it serves as a one-stop shopping destination for more than 22 million shoppers that visit annually. STC has been a central fixture in the Scarborough district Toronto since it opened in 1973.

My Mom emigrated to Canada in 1971, my Dad in 1975.

Before they knew each other, they lived on opposite ends of the same crescent in Scarborough. My Dad, and his motorcycle buddies would ride through the neighbourhood, revving their engines, while my Grandpa — before he was my Grandpa — cursed their noise.

Roots is a Canadian brand that is known for its comfort and quality, it has been around since 1973, the same year that STC opened. These quiet coincidences and overlapping timelines tell a story about a place where new beginnings took root — where families like mine built lives from scratch, where malls like STC weren't just places to shop, but common spaces to work, create memories, and meet people you didn't know you were going to know forever.


I took this sweater from my Dad when I moved to University in 2022.

I liked it because it was thin, comfortable, and oversized. I don't care much for hockey. This sweater, like many things in my family's story, is something that has been passed down (or stolen), worn through different phases of life, and still holds up.

There's something about the "V-stitch" at the neck and the frayed cuffs of this sweatshirt that makes it feel like home. It's a relic from the 90s, back when I was probably following the game much more closely, but now it has transitioned into a pure comfort piece.

I love that the logo is subtle and off to the side rather than splashed across the chest. It's the kind of sweatshirt that's been through hundreds of washes, making the fleece inside almost non-existent but the overall feel incredibly soft. It's my ultimate "do nothing" shirt-- the one I pull on for a late-night movie or a rainy afternoon when I want to feel completley at ease. The fraying doesn't feel like damage; it feels like a record of all the year I've kept this in my rotation.
